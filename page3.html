Redlining in New Orleans

This project incorporates the Home Owners’Loan Corporation (HOLC)historical redlining spatial data with contemporary datasets for food access, public works investments and healthcare access. HOLC districts were divided into four sections ranked A-D - A was ranked the best where white, “non-immigrants” were prevalent, whereas D, people of color and low income were prevalent. GIS analysis was conducted to uncover potential correlations between present day investment and historical redlining districts. Outcomes suggest Healthcare locations and food access are typical of both C and D redling rank geographies whereas investment per square mile was greater in B and C redlining rank geographies.

Drought Analysis & Implications for Ethiopian Labor Migration

This remote sensing project considers anomalies in NDVI values across years 2000 - 2010 to ascertain patterns of known drought during this temporal timeframe within Ethiopia. Negative value pixels show drought anomaly in 2015 most accutely. While there are areas/regions of contiguous negative values representing significant drought impact to vegetative cover, a particular 'hotspot' is within the Addis Ababa vicinity. Discussion of the findings relative to labor migration, urbanization and crop and resource stress is conducted in light of the project's detected NDVI anomalies.

GIS Analysis of China's Belt and Road Initiative

The Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) is China’s massive $4 trillion infrastructure and development project to promote economic cooperation among countries in Europe and Asia, with a goal of connecting the entire world through land and see. Part of this initiative involves the revitalization of a Silk Road Economic Belt as well as a 21st Century Maritime Silk Road. Since the initial announcement in 2013, China has undertaken several construction projects to see this vision to reality. To do so, China has been taking big risks in making investments across the world, which includes areas that may not be economically developed. The purpose of this spatial analysis is twofold: visualize in small scale the global implications of the project in its entirety; and in larger scale, conduct a cost path analysis for certain segments of the project that may pose risk to the BRI project assumptions.

Intergenerational Income Mobility for Detroit Metro

This spatial analysis is part of an ongoing study exploring the relationship between intergenerational mobility and changing characteristics of post-industrial cities of the Midwest. For cities like Detroit, these changes taking in the form of depopulation alongside gentrification and renewal of the urban core. Using spatial statistical tools provided by GIS, the relationship is explored for Detroit metropolitan. Two novel factors (social capital and family structure) are modeled to reveal the spatial dimension of the relationship these variables have with mobility outcomes. Challenges in finding a robust equation are also discussed, along with a discussion of the various tests carried out in the process.

Potential Watershed Contamination due to Mineral Extraction in Afghanistan

Afghanistan’s lack of clean water has had detrimental health effects on the population, most acutely on children. The past three decades of war have further impacted water and sanitation infrastructure. In the capital, Kabul, 80% of the population lacks access to clean drinking water. Across the county, 25% of the deaths among children under 5 are from contaminated water. While the reasons for these deficiencies are complex and numerous, this spatial analysis focuses more narrowly on extractive industries as potential threat water supplies, utilizing Afganistan's 34 provinces as the project's unit of anlysis. Generally, the project assumes that increased aggregate extractive potential equates more water supply hazard.
